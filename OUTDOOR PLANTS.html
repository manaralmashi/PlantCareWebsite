Outdoor plants

Water the plant when the top
1 to 1 inch of soil feels dry to the
touch. Ficus elastica prefer
consistent moisture, but it is
necessary to let the soil dry out
slightly between watering to
prevent overwatering and root
rot.

Ficus elastica prefers bright, indirect
light. When used as a potted indoor
plant, its leaves may burn in hot
sunlight, high temperature or
poor ventilation, so shade it properly
In weak sunlight in winter, place
it in a place with relatively strong
sunlight to ensure brighter colored
leaves

Ficus elastica grown in the garden
do not require much fertilizer;
Apply compound fertilizer 3 times
a year for potted plants, and in
addition to the basic fertilizer
in agricultural soil, apply
compound fertilizer once every
two weeks to support growth.
Use low concentration fertilizers
frequently to avoid burning the
roots

Yucca gigantea should be watered
every few weeks during the active
growing season between spring and
summer, and less frequently in the
fall and winter months. Excessive
watering can be detrimental to
this species. Yellowing or browning
of the lower leaves is a sign of
overwatering. Allow the soil to dry
completely between waterings to
prevent root rot.

Yucca gigantea thrives with partial
sun exposure and can tolerate full
sun in cool weather. However, they
are more susceptible to sunburn,
as they cannot withstand the
intense sunlight in extremely
hot environments

Yucca gigantea should be fertilized
with diluted aloe vera fertilizer
every two to three months. It is
recommended to use commercial
cactus fertilizer or fertilizers
containing a 12-6-19 formula of
nitrogen, phosphorus and potassium
for this.

In normal time, a mist of water can
be sprayed towards the leaves to
increase the surrounding humidity
and prevent burrs Leaves from
drying out. It should be noted that
the dracaena angustifolia plant is
sensitive to fluoride in tap water
and dark spots or yellow edges may
form on its leaves when tap water
is used over the long term, so it
is recommended to use collected
rainwater or purified water.

Dracaena angustifolia needs plenty
of indirect, filtered, or dim light when
grown indoors, so it should be kept
in a sunny location all day. Of course,
the number of naturally occurring
daylight hours can vary depending
on the season, but a good rule of
thumb is at least 6 hours, including
up to 6-33 hours of direct sunlight
per day.

Using a liquid household plant
fertilizer throughout the spring
and into early fall is the best way
to get your dracaena angustifolia
to achieve optimal growth results.
You only need? To a teaspoon once
a month of a fertilizer rich in
potassium and iron. Overfeeding
dracaena angustifolia can cause
damage to its growth and cause
the leaves to turn brown

Ficus benjamina should be watered
regularly between spring and
fall during theactive growing
season.Overwatering and
underwatering can cause the
leaves of this species to fall
to the ground.It should be
planted in a well-drained
pot Allow the soil to dry
partially between watering

The Ficus benjamina prefers
to grow in a location where it
gets light for some of the day
but also enjoys occasional
shade. While they can tolerate
constant exposure to light or
constant shade, either condition
may affect their health,
appearing as either leaf burn
from overexposure or stunted
growth due to light deprivation

Ficus benjamina are heavy
feeders and need fertilizer
during the active growing
season between spring and
fall. A balanced fertilizer
- 10-10 or 32-1 - of nitrogen,
phosphorus and potassium
can be used to feed this
species
